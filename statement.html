Artist Statement

In the beginning of my artistic journey, I focused heavily on character design without much thought behind it. First it would start with an idea, influenced by the popular media that surrounded me. That idea would come to life through a simple pencil sketch and for years that was my process. Although it was great practice, I started to shift my artistic vision into wanting to bring my sketches to life. I achieved this by focusing on pictorial pieces, with an emphasis on watercolor on wood as my medium of choice. It was during this time that I would experiment with creating art in the digital space. Digital illustrations are typically where most artists start when getting into the technological realm. It was no different for me. As I entered college, I wanted to explore the digital space more. All of my pieces are experimental as I attempt new techniques. However, one thing that ties all of my works together, both the pictorial pieces from before and the digital creations of today, is the concept. A great majority of my pieces focus on mental health and a few are even based on my own experiences and interests.
It is important that my works are somewhat relevant to my own experiences because I feel that it allows me as the artist to get closer to the audience. There is something personal and raw about sharing my feelings and experiences without being completely obvious. It is the little influences from my personal life that makes the pieces more powerful. For example, in the game I created titled In Reality, a lot of it was based on my own feelings. It felt real which is something that I strive for with a lot of my personal work. In future works, I would like to stick with using myself as inspiration for my pieces; however, I want to create them in a broader narrative. My goals beyond my college career include diving into the gaming world which I have already started to experiment with. In that space, I think my ideas would come alive more thoroughly than I have been able to achieve in the past because of the added narrative of gameplay on top of the theme. Even in my first attempt at game creation, I felt like my ideas were realized in a way I have always envisioned.
My artistic style has always taken influences from anime and cartoons (including games with that style). Even in my pictorial works, my style stays consistent. In terms of my works as a whole, they tend to be on the darker side because I talk about darker things. Some of my pieces though, were created solely for the purpose of experimenting with different techniques. Those ones tend to be happier. They still hold my own influence though because they are based on my personal interests.
Overall, I am proud of what I was able to showcase, especially knowing where I started from. I believe that my works are only going to get better as I become more experienced as a digital artist.
